Reports and coordinates

Each source statement is retained separately before conservative entity merging. Dates retain their precision. Four evidence-reviewed report aliases are now merged, leaving 8,391 assertions and 7,745 conservative catalog entities. This is not a claim of 7,745 physically distinct formations.

Location roles are deliberately separated. There are 419 field candidates or sites (409 candidate fields, four corroborated fields, and six registered sites), 3,886 approximate locality references, and 3,440 unresolved reports. Fifty-seven field-level records are explicit reviewed overrides; 384 candidate rows derive from publisher map targets, with overlap where a target was reviewed. Locality references help users find reports; they are not displayed as exact fields. Every accepted site records its method, uncertainty, evidence, review status, and rights status independently.

The bounded source expansion preserves source geography and leaves 167 alias and 83 probable-overlap candidates unmerged; 449 new normalized keys are not claimed as 449 proven-new formations.

The worldwide image archive exposes 7,956 unique image links through 8,109 report-image relationships across 1,929 reports. It contains 567 US image links, 7,325 links attached to known non-US reports, and 64 whose country remains unknown. This release includes 54 live-checked, rights-gated Crop Circle Archives image links tied by place, date, and report context to 23 US formations. Of 707 US rough-locality reports, 229 currently have public catalog image records; those photos do not make the locality centroids exact. The 7,398 global publisher image URLs were enumerated from report pages but not independently image-fetched and remain clearly labeled unverified and rights-gated. Image links are evidence records, not claims that every photograph has been geolocated or registered.

US-first field-resolution campaign

The campaign ranks US reports by date precision, source-photo availability, road/farm/airport clues, and imagery coverage. Candidate discovery can be semi-automated, but a field is not accepted automatically. The primary evidence sources are USGS DOQ, USGS HRO, USGS Aerial Photo Single Frames, and USGS-distributed NAIP, supplemented by USDA's NAIP program guidance, historical-film holdings, and aerial-imagery ordering guide.

A standard registration uses 8–12 well-distributed control points plus independent checkpoints that are not used to solve the transform. Residuals, checkpoint error, source acquisition date, and uncertainty are recorded. Google Earth is used only for manual comparison under its historical-imagery guidance and imagery-date guidance; it is not scraped as a bulk source. When the evidence cannot isolate a field, the record remains unresolved.

Straight components and orientation

  1. A straight-component candidate is an image-space detection.
  2. A diagram angle is measured in an image whose north direction may be unknown.
  3. A true azimuth is a geographic bearing clockwise from true north.
  4. Only a reviewed true azimuth with evidence and uncertainty can create an atlas projection.

The registration lab can solve a projective transform from four or more paired landmarks, but the campaign standard is 8–12 controls plus independent checkpoints. Two clicks along a component then produce a traceable true-north bearing, uncertainty, and export package.

The evidence qualifies the local orientation, not its extension across hundreds of kilometres. Every long-distance line is labeled experimental and has no demonstrated predictive validity.

Alignment tests

Corridor hits use spherical cross-track and along-track distances. Statistical eligibility also requires origin, target-coordinate, and distance-dependent angular uncertainty to fit inside the corridor. All current results remain exploratory until the range, corridor, directionality, date ordering, quality rules, null model, and multiple-testing correction are preregistered. Useful nulls preserve spatial density, source coverage, country, and year.

The earlier build reported 16 centerline-corridor matches against rough locality centroids. Those are not formation sites and are now excluded. Accepted rays tested against the two currently alignment-eligible sites produce zero corridor matches. The other 417 site/candidate records, unresolved reports, and locality references are excluded by the current quality gate.

Image rights

Local image registration never uploads the selected file. Source-linked images are visible in each report's archive, but only images with defensible registration evidence receive map footprints. Each rights-restricted source photograph remains off by default and loads directly from the source website only after the user asks to see it. The atlas publishes the URL, hash, corners, and review metadata but does not copy or redistribute those pixels. A failed or blocked remote request remains failed closed.

The current KML/KMZ packages zero image files. It carries 25 rights-compatible remote-linked GroundOverlays that point to source-hosted photographs and may fail if a host blocks the request. Remote loading fails closed unless an overlay record explicitly authorizes it; rights-gated source records remain links or reviewed footprints. Open-license records retain creator and license attribution.
